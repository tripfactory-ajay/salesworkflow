1.2
Ground reality disclosure — 14 items
Hotels · rooms · meals · timings — before any payment
Sales Expert Booking Owner STF
›
14 mandatory group disclosures — customer must sign each:
  • Hotels may be outside city centre / highway zone
  • Triple rooms may be double + sofa/extra bed
  • Some meals may be packed (early starts, long drives)
  • Group timings fixed — individual flexibility limited
  • Walking required at many European sites
  • Transfers are common group — private is chargeable
  • Early check-in / late checkout not guaranteed
  • Flights may have layovers; airline may change
  • Optionals cost extra unless written in package
  • Room upgrades / views not guaranteed
  • Jain / special dietary must be pre-requested
  • Coach access restricted at some attractions
  • Tour Manager runs the group — no private customisation
  • Special requests need written approval — not auto-guaranteed
Missing acknowledgement = Sales-side failure.

🚌 Driver / Supplier
MGR Finance Manager (approves over-threshold) SUP Ops Controller (verifies expenses) STF Finance Executive (records & settles) STF Driver / Supplier Rep
4.2
Driver / supplier float issued pre-tour
Float advanced (e.g. €500) · recorded in LERMS · driver signs receipt
DriverFinance ExecutiveOps ControllerSTFSUP
›
Float issue process:
  • Ops Controller (SUP) submits float request: tour code, driver name, amount, justification
  • Finance Manager (MGR) approves amounts above standard float threshold
  • Driver signs float receipt — amount, date, tour code, return-by date
  • Finance Executive (STF) records in LERMS: "Advanced to Supplier — [Tour Code] — [Driver Name]"
  • Zero float advances without Ops Controller authorisation
Typical float: €300–€500 depending on tour type and route. Multi-day tours may have higher float — Finance Manager approval required above standard limit.
4.3
Driver submits expense invoice post-tour
Receipts + invoice within 48 hrs · verified against float · LERMS closed
DriverOps Controller (verifies)Finance Executive (settles)STFSUP
›
Driver submits within 48 hours of tour end:
  • Itemised expense invoice
  • Original receipts (or clear photos) — no receipt = expense rejected
  • Signed LERMS return form
Approved expense categories: Parking · road tolls above agreed daily cap · fuel top-up if applicable · pre-approved emergency repairs · ferry/tunnel if not in contract.
Not reimbursable: Meals/accommodation where included · personal expenses · anything without a receipt.

Settlement flow:
  • Ops Controller (SUP) verifies: categories match approved list · receipts match amounts · tour dates correct
  • Finance Executive (STF) reconciles: actual vs float advanced
  • Under float: Driver returns balance. Ops Controller records in LERMS.
  • Over float (within 10%): Ops Controller approves → Finance pays difference
  • Over float (above 10%): Finance Manager (MGR) sign-off required
  • Missing receipts: That line item rejected — no exceptions
Finance Executive marks float "Settled" in LERMS with actual vs advanced summary. Disputes escalated to Ops Manager → Finance Manager within 5 working days.

B2B exception: Agent credit terms may allow net settlement post-tour — must be within credit limit and in signed agreement. Finance Manager confirms before file proceeds.

⚙ Ops & Sales Control
MGR Business Head (Level 3) MGR Management (Level 2) SUP Ops & Sales Control Desk (Level 1)

Escalation — §24 · 3 levels · all booking types
ESCALATION›
Level 1 (SUP): Booking Owner / Consultant / Account Manager → Ops & Sales Control Desk · 24-hour window
Level 2 (MGR): Control Desk → Management · 4-hour escalation if unresolved
Level 3 (MGR): Business Head decision — final, documented on file

Common triggers: Passport not in hand T-45 · Flight not sealed T-30 · Outstanding dues T-14 · Visa rejection · Dirty file at handover · Driver float not settled within 48hrs of tour end · Agent missing document submission
📊
Booking Owner scorecard — §25 · seasonal review
SEASONAL›
Tracks per Booking Owner / FIT Consultant / B2B Account Manager: booking errors (type + frequency) · late payment collections · dirty handovers · customer complaints attributable to booking stage · escalations generated · gate violations (A–G) · driver float disputes attributable to booking · B2B: agent credit incidents. Persistent violations → managed performance review by Ops Manager and HR.
⊘
Non-negotiable violations — §26 · zero tolerance
ZERO TOLERANCE›
  • Unrecorded discount or special price given to customer
  • Verbal inclusion commitment not in writing
  • Booking marked confirmed without payment received
  • Dirty or incomplete file pushed to Operations
  • Booking Owner responsibility transferred without documented hand-off note
  • Agent booking confirmed without signed written terms
  • No visa feasibility confirmed for late Europe booking
  • Vouchers released with outstanding dues
  • Special request guaranteed to customer without written approval on file
  • No ground reality disclosure before deposit collected
  • Driver float issued without LERMS entry and signed receipt
  • Driver expense reimbursed without original receipt
